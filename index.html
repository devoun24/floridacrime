Crime in the Sunshine State

By Devoun Cetoute

474,481 crimes have been commited and reported by sheriffs' offices or county police departments from across Florida last year, according to the FBI's Uniform Crime Reporting Program. Crime is an event that will affect every citizen at some point in their lives. For some residents in the state that time will be sooner than later. This special project will show you what Florida cities are hotspots for criminal activity, see if the high amount of crime can be attributed to social factors and see how Gainesville compares to cities with similar crime rates.
All crime data has been gathered from the FBI and social data from the United States Census Bureau.

Criminal Hotspots

The Reason Behind Crime

The FBI website tells us to be careful when ranking total crime.

"These incomplete analyses have often created misleading perceptions which adversely affect geographic entities and their residents," according to FBI documentation.

They go on to explain that rankings ignore the uniqueness of each area and don't take in-account the social aspects that could be affecting that area. Some of these aspects can be urbanization, economics, modes of transportation and climate.

How Does The Swamp Measure Up?

Every city has its fair share of crime and Gainesville is no exception. In fact, when compared to similar cities, those that have a population between 100,000 and 150,000, the swamp is at the top of the charts.

As we just learned, comparing cities to each other can be misleading. Gainesville does have one of the highest citizens in poverty rate in the state.
